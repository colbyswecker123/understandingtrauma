Support resources

When you need more than a note.

A reminder can help, but support matters too. Use this page as a gentle starting point when you need grounding or a next step.

If you are in immediate danger

Please contact local emergency services or reach out to a trusted person near you right now. This site cannot provide emergency help.

Talk to someone safe

Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, pastor, mentor, counselor, or someone nearby who can sit with you.

Consider professional support

A licensed counselor, therapist, doctor, or local clinic can help you work through heavier moments with care and structure.

Use your environment

Move to a safer, calmer space if you can. Turn on a light, get water, sit against a wall, or step outside for fresh air.

Make a tiny plan

Choose one next step: breathe, text someone, drink water, shower, journal, pray, walk, or rest for five minutes.

Grounding step

Name five things you can see, four things you can feel, three things you can hear, two things you can smell, and one thing you can taste.

Breathing step

Try a slow inhale for four counts, hold for two, and exhale for six. Repeat gently without forcing it.

Connection step

Text someone safe and simple: “I am having a hard moment. Can you sit with me?”
